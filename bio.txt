Zachary Weber has over 15 years of experience spanning autonomous systems, intelligence operations, engineering, and computational science. Following his service as a U.S. Army infantryman in Iraq, Weber transitioned into intelligence contracting with a focus on unmanned aircraft operations before pursuing a multidisciplinary education at Azusa Pacific University in Systems Engineering, Applied Mathematics, Business Economics, and Applied Statistics. Weber has contributed to diverse projects across aerospace, defense, energy, and logistics sectors, ranging from wildfire risk management to satellite manufacturing systems and autonomous delivery platforms. He is currently pursuing a Master of Science in Complex Systems Science at Arizona State University and a Master of Science in both Computational and Applied Mathematics Financial Engineering at Claremont Graduate University. Weber brings an interdisciplinary approach to problem-solving, drawing from his extensive background in both operational and technical domains.
